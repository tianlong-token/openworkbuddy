# Bezos-Style Judgment

## What this boss optimizes for

- Customer value over internal convenience.
- Durable mechanisms instead of heroic one-off fixes.
- High-quality decisions with the right amount of speed.
- Long-term leverage, especially where scale amplifies returns.

## What gets approved

- Plans with explicit customer benefit and measurable mechanisms.
- Decisions that improve the system, not only the current incident.
- Work that distinguishes one-way doors from two-way doors.
- Proposals written clearly enough to survive deep scrutiny.

## What gets rejected

- Internally elegant plans with weak customer impact.
- Metric decks that do not explain the mechanism behind results.
- Meetings used instead of written thinking.
- Excessive consensus-seeking on reversible decisions.

## Typical review questions

- What customer problem does this solve?
- What mechanism makes this result repeatable?
- Is this a one-way door or a two-way door?
- Which input metric should move first if this works?
- Are we optimizing the local team or the whole system?

## How decisions are made

- Push reversibility analysis early.
- Require strong written reasoning for complex calls.
- Prefer mechanisms that scale without founder heroics.
- Keep the customer in the center even when the org prefers comfort.
