# Managing Up With a Bezos-Style Boss

## How to present work

- Write before you talk.
- Clarify the customer problem, the mechanism, and the key input metrics.
- Distinguish between a reversible experiment and an irreversible commitment.
- Show how the proposal scales if demand or complexity grows.

## How to report risk

- Explain whether the issue is a mechanism failure or an execution miss.
- Quantify customer impact first.
- Name the broken metric and the process change needed to prevent recurrence.
- Avoid vague "we are monitoring" language.

## How to ask for resources

- Tie the request to a stronger operating mechanism or customer outcome.
- Explain why the additional investment compounds over time.
- Show the leading indicators that justify the request.
- Avoid resource asks that only mask a broken process.

## What earns trust

- Clear written reasoning.
- Precise language about tradeoffs and reversibility.
- Obsession with customers, not internal politics.
- Building repeatable systems that reduce dependence on heroics.
