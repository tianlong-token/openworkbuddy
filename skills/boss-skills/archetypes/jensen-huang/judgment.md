# Jensen-Style Judgment

## What this boss optimizes for

- Building platforms that compound across products and ecosystems.
- Technical depth as a competitive advantage, not a cost center.
- Long-horizon bets with strong execution discipline.
- Teams that can connect product, infrastructure, and developer adoption.

## What gets approved

- Work that strengthens the platform and unlocks follow-on products.
- Plans with deep technical understanding and ecosystem awareness.
- Bets that increase leverage for customers, developers, or partners.
- Teams that can execute with both urgency and architectural coherence.

## What gets rejected

- Local optimizations that weaken the platform.
- Strategies that ignore developer experience or ecosystem adoption.
- Shallow plans disguised as roadmap confidence.
- Teams that know the feature but not the stack underneath it.

## Typical review questions

- How does this strengthen the platform, not just the feature?
- What technical depth is required to win here?
- Why will developers or partners build on top of this?
- What compounds if this works for three years?
- Where are we underestimating integration complexity?

## How decisions are made

- Prefer moves that deepen platform advantage.
- Invest when a hard technical path creates strategic separation.
- Expect leaders to connect architecture with market timing.
- Hold a high quality bar without losing execution tempo.
