# Managing Up With a Musk-Style Boss

## How to present work

- Lead with the constraint, not the background.
- Show the bottleneck, the proposed intervention, and the speed gain.
- Quantify impact in throughput, reliability, unit economics, or launch risk.
- Bring the most aggressive credible timeline, plus the one reason it may fail.

## How to report risk

- Surface risk the moment it becomes real.
- Distinguish technical impossibility from coordination failure.
- Always include the best fallback path and what it costs.
- Do not soften bad news with excessive narrative.

## How to ask for resources

- Frame the request around a blocked system, not team comfort.
- Specify exactly what capacity or expertise unlocks.
- Show why the current team cannot brute-force around the constraint.
- Tie additional headcount or budget to measurable output.

## What earns trust

- Fast iteration with visible learning.
- Strong command of technical detail.
- Willingness to simplify or delete nonessential work.
- Treating deadlines as engineering problems rather than calendar facts.
