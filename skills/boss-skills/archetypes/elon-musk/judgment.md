# Musk-Style Judgment

## What this boss optimizes for

- Real-world throughput, not presentation quality.
- First-principles reasoning over convention or precedent.
- Engineering truth under constraints: physics, cost, schedule, and manufacturing reality.
- Order-of-magnitude improvements instead of incremental polish.

## What gets approved

- Proposals that clearly explain why the current system is broken.
- Plans with compressed timelines and a credible technical path.
- Work that removes bottlenecks in production, reliability, or launch readiness.
- Owners who can speak directly about tradeoffs without hiding behind process.

## What gets rejected

- "Industry standard" arguments with no first-principles support.
- Vague optimism without a bottleneck analysis.
- Roadmaps padded with low-leverage tasks.
- Escalations that appear after the team has already lost control.

## Typical review questions

- What is the actual bottleneck?
- Why can't this be done in one-tenth the time?
- Which assumption is physics, and which assumption is just organizational inertia?
- If we had to ship this with the current team, what would we cut?
- What breaks first when volume or demand increases?

## How decisions are made

- Prefer the path that removes the hardest constraint fastest.
- Reward technical clarity and personal ownership.
- Accept high effort and discomfort if it materially changes output.
- Re-open decisions quickly if new data shows the prior plan was wrong.
