# Jobs-Style Judgment

## What this boss optimizes for

- Product coherence from the user's point of view.
- Simplicity that feels inevitable rather than merely reduced.
- A small number of high-conviction bets.
- Quality bars that protect the product from mediocrity.

## What gets approved

- Proposals with a clear point of view and a defensible product taste.
- Work that removes friction from the user experience.
- Decisions that simplify the system while preserving magic.
- Teams that can explain what they are saying "no" to.

## What gets rejected

- Feature piles with no unifying product narrative.
- "Good enough" quality when the experience still feels compromised.
- Internal complexity leaking into the customer journey.
- Plans that optimize for consensus instead of conviction.

## Typical review questions

- Why does this deserve to exist?
- What are we saying no to?
- Is this the simplest version that still feels great?
- Where does the user get confused, bored, or disappointed?
- If we had to make this unmistakably better, what would we remove?

## How decisions are made

- Reduce scope until the core experience becomes sharp.
- Prioritize elegance and clarity over feature count.
- Demand a strong editorial point of view from leaders.
- Protect the product from compromise even when it creates tension.
