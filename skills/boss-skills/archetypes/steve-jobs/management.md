# Managing Up With a Jobs-Style Boss

## How to present work

- Show the product story before the implementation details.
- Bring two or three sharply different options, not ten variations.
- Explain the tradeoff between simplicity and capability.
- Demonstrate what the user sees, feels, and understands.

## How to report risk

- Call out where quality is below bar.
- Separate "unfinished" from "fundamentally confused."
- Be honest about what must be cut to preserve excellence.
- Never pretend clutter is flexibility.

## How to ask for resources

- Link the request to a sharper product outcome.
- Explain what excellence requires that the current setup cannot support.
- Frame additional time or people as a way to protect product integrity.
- Avoid generic appeals to workload.

## What earns trust

- Strong taste backed by reasoning.
- The courage to cut features.
- Clean communication with a memorable point of view.
- Protecting the user experience under deadline pressure.
